The pain-points of Web3 onboarding

Challenges

We have made a list of Web3's biggest user onboarding challenges.

Formatting

Ethereum addresses are difficult to differentiate from each other and lack creative identifiers which can be found in conventional usernames.

Private keys

Private keys are difficult to remember. People do not like things that are difficult.

Centralization

Current solutions provide Web3 onboarding with server-side private key management. Although some parties claim non-custodial key management, they still centrally govern the handling of the data.

Incompatibility

Current solutions are bound to limitations in terms of interoperability. All devices and browsers should be supported.

Solution

A brief overview of our Web3 onboarding protocol

Open Source

All our apps are open source and free to download from GitHub. We'd love to see contributors join dAuth.

Encrypted

Private keys are encrypted before they are stored locally. Encryption is done by user-input in the form of a password.

Interoperability

dAuth is compatible with most devices and operating systems. Mobile support to be added in the future.

Non-custodial key management

Private keys are generated locally. dAuth does not make any direct requests to centralized servers. It is a protocol.

Minimal wallet interaction

In order to increase Web3 adoption, a familiar user interface is required. We have avoided unnecessary interactions.

Decentralized

Because dAuth is more of a protocol than an application, it is completely serverless and thus decentralized.
